        Data Caveats & Methodology

        Fiber Penetration

        Shows current fiber coverage from FCC BDC data. Red = low penetration (more unserved homes, greater opportunity). Green = high penetration (already well-served by fiber).

        Demographics Score

        Composite score based on: median household income (ARPU potential), population growth, housing density (build efficiency), work-from-home rates, and owner-occupancy. Green = positive profile for fiber investment.

        Market Attractiveness

        Combines demographics + fiber opportunity. Counties ranked into thirds: Most Attractive (top 33%), Neutral (middle 33%), Least Attractive (bottom 33%). Higher scores = better demographics AND more unserved homes.

        BEAD Funding

        NTIA BEAD county-level award data is not yet publicly available. Status shown as "Unverified" pending official release. Layer currently inactive.

        Competition & Build Momentum

        Competition intensity derived from FCC FTTP location data — counts distinct fiber providers per county (0-3 scale). Momentum requires two BDC filing periods; currently pending Dec 2024 data release.

        Terrain & Build Difficulty

        Terrain roughness derived from USGS 3DEP elevation data. Combined with USDA Rural-Urban Continuum Codes (RUCC) to estimate construction cost tiers. Green = easy/flat terrain, Red = challenging/mountainous.

        NPV Calculator

        Simplified financial model for fiber build feasibility. Assumes 40% OpEx ratio, uniform subscriber acquisition, and constant ARPU. BEAD subsidy offsets CapEx. Results are indicative only—verify with detailed engineering studies.

        Data Limitations

        FCC data relies on provider self-reporting and may overstate coverage. Build costs (terrain, permits, labor) are estimated. MO fiber data sourced from FCC BDC FTTP location-level records (Jun 2025). BEAD county-level awards and momentum (Dec 2024 delta) pending data availability. Verify specific markets before investment decisions.

        Data sources: FCC BDC (Jun 2025) | Census ACS 5-year (2018, 2023) | TIGER/Line (2023) | NTIA BEAD | USGS 3DEP | USDA RUCC

        BEAD State Funding Tracker

        Estimated allocations from NTIA BEAD program (broadbandusa.ntia.doc.gov). $/Location = state allocation ÷ total eligible locations. County-level awards are implied estimates only.

        FiberMap USA

        Institutional-Grade Fiber Market Intelligence. Built for Operators and Investors.

        What It Is

        FiberMap USA is an interactive market intelligence platform that maps every fiber, cable, and fixed wireless broadband market in the United States at the county level. It aggregates data from the FCC, Census Bureau, USDA, and USGS into a single decision-ready tool — built specifically for the people deploying capital into fiber infrastructure.

        Why It Was Built

        The US fiber buildout is entering its most competitive phase. Operators and investors are making multi-million dollar decisions about where to build, where to overbuild, and where to avoid — often with fragmented, hard-to-use public data or expensive research subscriptions that weren't designed for the field.

        FiberMap USA was built to close that gap: real FCC broadband data, real demographics, real terrain intelligence — in one place, at a fraction of the cost of legacy research products.

        Who It's For

        • Fiber overbuilders and regional ISPs evaluating new build markets
        • Private equity and infrastructure investors assessing acquisition targets and competitive dynamics
        • Strategy and BD teams at larger carriers benchmarking their footprint against competitors

        Key Features

        Interactive County Map — All 50 States

        Switch between 8 map layers: Fiber Penetration, Cable Coverage, Fixed Wireless, Market Attractiveness, Demographics, Competition Intensity, Build Momentum, and Build Difficulty.

        Provider Footprint View

        Select any major fiber operator — AT&T, Verizon Fios, Frontier, Lumen, Brightspeed, Metronet, and 20+ others — and instantly see their county-level footprint across the entire US. Counties are shaded by depth of coverage, showing where a provider has dense penetration vs. a thin presence. National passing totals are reconciled to Q4 2025 public earnings for every operator.

        Competitive Overlap Screen

        Select up to 5 providers simultaneously in Competition View. Each operator is assigned a distinct color, and counties where two or more providers overlap are highlighted in yellow — making overbuild exposure immediately visible. A configurable minimum share threshold ensures only meaningful competitive presence is flagged, not nominal FCC filings.

        Market Attractiveness Score

        A proprietary composite score weighing household income, housing density, population growth, work-from-home rates, and remaining buildable opportunity. Counties already saturated with fiber rank lower — reflecting real build economics, not just demographics.

        Build Momentum Layer

        Powered by FCC Broadband Data Collection filings. Shows actual 6-month fiber location growth (Dec 2024 → Jun 2025) at the county level — so you can see where operators are actively building vs. where buildout has stalled.

        County Deep Dive

        Click any county for a full data panel: fiber penetration, unserved locations, demographics, terrain difficulty, competitive intensity, build momentum, and BEAD eligibility context.

        County Rankings Table

        Every county in America, sortable and filterable by state, momentum class, or any metric. Select individual counties and export to CSV for offline analysis.

        Shareable Views

        Every map state — layer, provider, county selection, competitive screen — is encoded in the URL. Send a link and your counterpart sees exactly what you're looking at.
